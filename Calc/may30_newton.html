Newton's Method · Ch.13.4
Calc A2 Final 23 June 2026
Core Formula
\[\mathbf{x}_{n+1} = \mathbf{x}_n - [DF(\mathbf{x}_n)]^{-1}\,F(\mathbf{x}_n)\]
2×2 inverse: \(\begin{pmatrix}a&b\\c&d\end{pmatrix}^{-1}=\dfrac{1}{ad-bc}\begin{pmatrix}d&-b\\-c&a\end{pmatrix}\)  ·  Differentiable if all partial derivatives are continuous  ·  Linear approx: \(F(\mathbf{x}_0+\mathbf{h})\approx F(\mathbf{x}_0)+[DF(\mathbf{x}_0)]\mathbf{h}\)
Problem 1 Two-Step Iteration & Convergence 12 pts
We want to solve \(F(x,y)=\mathbf{0}\) where
\[F(x,y)=\begin{pmatrix}x^2 - 2y \\ xy - 1\end{pmatrix}, \qquad \mathbf{x}_0 = (2,\,1).\]
  1. (a) Compute the Jacobian \([DF(x,y)]\) in general, then evaluate at \((2,1)\). Explain why \(F\) is differentiable everywhere. 3 pts
  2. (b) Evaluate \(F(2,1)\) and use linear approximation to estimate \(F(2.01,\,1.01)\). 2 pts
  3. (c) Perform one Newton step to find \(\mathbf{x}_1\). Compute \(F(\mathbf{x}_1)\) and confirm it is closer to \(\mathbf{0}\). 4 pts
  4. (d) Perform a second Newton step from \(\mathbf{x}_1\) to find \(\mathbf{x}_2\). The true solution satisfies \(x^3=2\). How close is \(\mathbf{x}_2\)? 3 pts
(a) Jacobian
\[[DF(x,y)]=\begin{pmatrix}2x & -2 \\ y & x\end{pmatrix}, \qquad [DF(2,1)]=\begin{pmatrix}4 & -2 \\ 1 & 2\end{pmatrix}.\] All entries are polynomials — continuous everywhere — so \(F\) is differentiable everywhere.
(b) Linear Approximation
\(F(2,1)=\begin{pmatrix}4-2\\2-1\end{pmatrix}=\begin{pmatrix}2\\1\end{pmatrix}\). With \(\mathbf{h}=(0.01,0.01)^T\):
\[F(2.01,1.01)\approx\begin{pmatrix}2\\1\end{pmatrix}+\begin{pmatrix}4&-2\\1&2\end{pmatrix}\begin{pmatrix}0.01\\0.01\end{pmatrix}=\begin{pmatrix}2.02\\1.03\end{pmatrix}.\]
(c) First Newton Step
\(\det[DF(2,1)]=4\cdot2-(-2)\cdot1=10.\) \[[DF(2,1)]^{-1}=\frac{1}{10}\begin{pmatrix}2&2\\-1&4\end{pmatrix}.\] \[[DF]^{-1}F(2,1)=\frac{1}{10}\begin{pmatrix}2&2\\-1&4\end{pmatrix}\begin{pmatrix}2\\1\end{pmatrix}=\frac{1}{10}\begin{pmatrix}6\\2\end{pmatrix}=\begin{pmatrix}0.6\\0.2\end{pmatrix}.\]
\[\mathbf{x}_1=(2,1)-(0.6,0.2)=(1.4,\;0.8).\]
Check: \(F(1.4,0.8)=\begin{pmatrix}0.36\\0.12\end{pmatrix}\). Norm \(\approx0.38\), far smaller than \(\|(2,1)^T\|\) ✓
(d) Second Newton Step
\([DF(1.4,0.8)]=\begin{pmatrix}2.8&-2\\0.8&1.4\end{pmatrix},\quad\det=3.92+1.6=5.52.\) \[[DF]^{-1}=\frac{1}{5.52}\begin{pmatrix}1.4&2\\-0.8&2.8\end{pmatrix}.\] Update: \(\frac{1}{5.52}\begin{pmatrix}0.744\\0.048\end{pmatrix}\approx\begin{pmatrix}0.1348\\0.0087\end{pmatrix}.\)
\[\mathbf{x}_2\approx(1.2652,\;0.7913).\]
True solution \(x=2^{1/3}\approx1.2599\). Error at \(\mathbf{x}_2\) is \(\approx0.005\) — quadratic convergence ✓
Problem 2 3D System — Newton in \(\mathbb{R}^3\) HARD 11 pts
Find intersection points of a sphere and two planes. Define \(F:\mathbb{R}^3\to\mathbb{R}^3\):
\[F(x,y,z)=\begin{pmatrix}x^2+y^2+z^2-3 \\ x+y-2 \\ y-z\end{pmatrix}, \qquad \mathbf{x}_0=(2,\,0,\,0).\]
  1. (a) Compute the \(3\times3\) Jacobian \([DF]\) and evaluate at \((2,0,0)\). 3 pts
  2. (b) Evaluate \(F(2,0,0)\). Is \((2,0,0)\) already a solution? 1 pt
  3. (c) Compute \([DF(2,0,0)]^{-1}\) by row-reducing \([A\,|\,I]\), then perform one Newton step to find \(\mathbf{x}_1\). 5 pts
  4. (d) Find all exact solutions of \(F=\mathbf{0}\). Which one is \(\mathbf{x}_1\) converging toward? 2 pts
(a) 3×3 Jacobian
\[[DF(x,y,z)]=\begin{pmatrix}2x&2y&2z\\1&1&0\\0&1&-1\end{pmatrix}, \qquad [DF(2,0,0)]=\begin{pmatrix}4&0&0\\1&1&0\\0&1&-1\end{pmatrix}.\]
(b) Value at guess
\[F(2,0,0)=\begin{pmatrix}4-3\\2-2\\0\end{pmatrix}=\begin{pmatrix}1\\0\\0\end{pmatrix}\neq\mathbf{0}.\] Not a solution — Newton must iterate.
(c) Inverse + Newton Step
\(A\) is lower triangular with \(\det=4\cdot1\cdot(-1)=-4\). Row reducing gives: \[A^{-1}=\begin{pmatrix}1/4&0&0\\-1/4&1&0\\-1/4&1&-1\end{pmatrix}.\] Update: \(A^{-1}\begin{pmatrix}1\\0\\0\end{pmatrix}=\begin{pmatrix}1/4\\-1/4\\-1/4\end{pmatrix}.\)
\[\mathbf{x}_1=\begin{pmatrix}2\\0\\0\end{pmatrix}-\begin{pmatrix}1/4\\-1/4\\-1/4\end{pmatrix}=\begin{pmatrix}7/4\\1/4\\1/4\end{pmatrix}=(1.75,\;0.25,\;0.25).\]
(d) All Exact Solutions
From \(y=z\) and \(x=2-y\), substitute into sphere: \((2-y)^2+2y^2=3\Rightarrow3y^2-4y+1=0\Rightarrow(3y-1)(y-1)=0.\)
Solutions: \(\bigl(\tfrac{5}{3},\tfrac{1}{3},\tfrac{1}{3}\bigr)\) and \((1,1,1)\).
\(\mathbf{x}_1=(1.75,0.25,0.25)\) is converging toward \((\tfrac53,\tfrac13,\tfrac13)\approx(1.667,0.333,0.333)\) ✓
Problem 3 Linear Approximation & IFT — When Newton Fails 7 pts
Consider \(F(x,y)=\begin{pmatrix}x^2+y^2 \\ 2xy\end{pmatrix}\). Test Newton's method at two starting points.
  1. (a) Compute \([DF(x,y)]\). Is \([DF(0,0)]\) invertible? What goes wrong if we try Newton at \((0,0)\)? 2 pts
  2. (b) State the IFT condition for local invertibility. Find all points where it fails for this \(F\). 3 pts
  3. (c) At \(\mathbf{x}_0=(2,1)\), compute one Newton step toward \(G(x,y)=F(x,y)-\begin{pmatrix}5\\4\end{pmatrix}=\mathbf{0}\). 2 pts
(a) Jacobian & Failure
\[[DF(x,y)]=\begin{pmatrix}2x&2y\\2y&2x\end{pmatrix}, \qquad [DF(0,0)]=\begin{pmatrix}0&0\\0&0\end{pmatrix}.\] \(\det=0\) — not invertible. The Newton step \(-[DF]^{-1}F\) is undefined at \((0,0)\); the method cannot proceed.
(b) IFT Condition
IFT requires \(\det[DF(\mathbf{x}_0)]\neq0\). Here \(\det[DF]=4x^2-4y^2=4(x-y)(x+y)\).
Fails on the lines \(x=y\) and \(x=-y\) — i.e. wherever \(|x|=|y|\).
(c) Newton Step at (2,1)
\([DG]=[DF]\). \(G(2,1)=F(2,1)-\begin{pmatrix}5\\4\end{pmatrix}=\begin{pmatrix}5\\4\end{pmatrix}-\begin{pmatrix}5\\4\end{pmatrix}=\begin{pmatrix}0\\0\end{pmatrix}.\)
\(G(2,1)=\mathbf{0}\) — already the exact solution. Newton terminates in 0 steps.
Verify: \(F(2,1)=\begin{pmatrix}4+1\\2\cdot2\cdot1\end{pmatrix}=\begin{pmatrix}5\\4\end{pmatrix}\) ✓
Problem 4 Polar Coordinates & Two-Step Iteration HARD 13 pts
Find the intersection of a circle and a parabola. Define
\[F(x,y)=\begin{pmatrix}x^2+y^2-4 \\ x - y^2 + 1\end{pmatrix}, \qquad \mathbf{x}_0=(1,\,1).\]
  1. (a) Compute \([DF(1,1)]\) and explain why \(F\) is differentiable at \((1,1)\). 3 pts
  2. (b) Use linear approximation to estimate \(F(1.02,\,0.99)\). 2 pts
  3. (c) Express \(F\) in polar coordinates. Compute \(D_rF\) and \(D_\theta F\) at \((r,\theta)=(\sqrt{2},\,\pi/4)\). 4 pts
  4. (d) Perform two Newton steps from \((1,1)\) to find \(\mathbf{x}_1\) and \(\mathbf{x}_2\). Is \(F(\mathbf{x}_2)\approx\mathbf{0}\)? 4 pts
(a) Jacobian at (1,1)
\[[DF(x,y)]=\begin{pmatrix}2x&2y\\1&-2y\end{pmatrix}, \qquad [DF(1,1)]=\begin{pmatrix}2&2\\1&-2\end{pmatrix}.\] All partial derivatives are polynomials, hence continuous everywhere — \(F\) differentiable at \((1,1)\).
(b) Linear Approximation
\(F(1,1)=\begin{pmatrix}-2\\1\end{pmatrix}\). With \(\mathbf{h}=(0.02,-0.01)^T\):
\[F(1.02,0.99)\approx\begin{pmatrix}-2\\1\end{pmatrix}+\begin{pmatrix}2&2\\1&-2\end{pmatrix}\begin{pmatrix}0.02\\-0.01\end{pmatrix}=\begin{pmatrix}-1.98\\1.04\end{pmatrix}.\]
(c) Polar Derivatives
With \(x=r\cos\theta,\;y=r\sin\theta\): \[F=\begin{pmatrix}r^2-4\\r\cos\theta-r^2\sin^2\theta+1\end{pmatrix}.\] \[D_rF=\begin{pmatrix}2r\\\cos\theta-2r\sin^2\theta\end{pmatrix}, \quad D_\theta F=\begin{pmatrix}0\\-r\sin\theta-2r^2\sin\theta\cos\theta\end{pmatrix}.\] At \((\sqrt{2},\pi/4)\) where \(\cos=\sin=\tfrac{1}{\sqrt2}\):
\[D_rF=\begin{pmatrix}2\sqrt{2}\\-1/\sqrt{2}\end{pmatrix}, \qquad D_\theta F=\begin{pmatrix}0\\-3\end{pmatrix}.\]
(d) Two Newton Steps
Step 1: \(\det[DF(1,1)]=2(-2)-2(1)=-6.\) \[[DF(1,1)]^{-1}=\frac{1}{-6}\begin{pmatrix}-2&-2\\-1&2\end{pmatrix}=\begin{pmatrix}1/3&1/3\\1/6&-1/3\end{pmatrix}.\] \[[DF]^{-1}F(1,1)=\begin{pmatrix}1/3&1/3\\1/6&-1/3\end{pmatrix}\begin{pmatrix}-2\\1\end{pmatrix}=\begin{pmatrix}-1/3\\-2/3\end{pmatrix}.\]
\[\mathbf{x}_1=(1,1)-(-1/3,-2/3)=(4/3,\;5/3)\approx(1.333,\;1.667).\]
Step 2: \(F(4/3,5/3)=\begin{pmatrix}5/9\\-4/9\end{pmatrix}.\) \([DF(4/3,5/3)]=\begin{pmatrix}8/3&10/3\\1&-10/3\end{pmatrix}\), \(\det=-110/9.\) Update \(\approx\begin{pmatrix}0.034\\0.143\end{pmatrix}.\)
\[\mathbf{x}_2\approx(1.299,\;1.524).\]
Check: \(F(1.299,1.524)\approx\begin{pmatrix}0.01\\-0.02\end{pmatrix}\approx\mathbf{0}\) ✓ Converging to a point on the circle \(x^2+y^2=4\).
