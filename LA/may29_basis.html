Problem 1  ·  10 points  ·  span & independence in \(\mathbb R^4\)

A robotic arm can push in the directions \(v_1=(1,2,0,1),\;v_2=(2,4,1,3),\;v_3=(3,6,1,4),\;v_4=(0,0,1,1)\) in its 4-D configuration space. The engineer wants to know how much of the space the arm can actually reach.

  1. Do \(v_1,\dots,v_4\) span all of \(\mathbb R^4\)? [2 pts]
  2. Find a basis for \(\operatorname{span}\{v_1,\dots,v_4\}\) and state its dimension. [3 pts]
  3. Are the four vectors linearly independent? If not, write each redundant vector as a combination of the others. [2 pts]
  4. Is \(w=(1,2,2,3)\) reachable (i.e. in the span)? [2 pts]
  5. If so, give the coordinates of \(w\) in your basis from (b). [1 pt]
Reveal answer
(a) Row-reduce the matrix with these as rows. \(R_2-2R_1=R_3-3R_1=R_4=(0,0,1,1)\), so only 2 pivots survive — they cannot span \(\mathbb R^4\) (you'd need 4 independent vectors). (b) A basis of the span is \(\{(1,2,0,1),\,(0,0,1,1)\}\); \(\dim=2\). (c) Dependent. From the reduction, \(v_2=2v_1+v_4\) and \(v_3=3v_1+v_4\) (and \(v_3-v_2=v_1\)). (d) Solve \(w=a(1,2,0,1)+b(0,0,1,1)\): first entry \(a=1\), third \(b=2\), check fourth \(a+b=3\)✓ and second \(2a=2\)✓. So yes, \(w=v_1+2v_4\). (e) Coordinates \((a,b)=(1,2)\).
Problem 2  ·  10 points  ·  basis of a solution space

A sensor network's consistency constraints are the homogeneous system \(A\mathbf x=\mathbf 0\) with \[A=\begin{pmatrix}1&2&-1&3\\2&4&-1&7\\1&2&0&4\end{pmatrix}.\] The valid sensor states form the null space — describe it completely.

  1. Solve \(A\mathbf x=\mathbf 0\) and give the general solution. [3 pts]
  2. Give a basis for \(\operatorname{Null}(A)\) and the nullity. [3 pts]
  3. State \(\operatorname{rank}(A)\) (count the pivots). [2 pts]
  4. Verify the rank-nullity bookkeeping for this matrix. [2 pts]
Reveal answer
(a) \(R_2-2R_1=(0,0,1,1)\), \(R_3-R_1=(0,0,1,1)\); clearing gives RREF \(\begin{pmatrix}1&2&0&4\\0&0&1&1\end{pmatrix}\). Pivots in columns 1, 3; free \(x_2,x_4\): \(x_1=-2x_2-4x_4,\; x_3=-x_4\). (b) Setting \((x_2,x_4)=(1,0)\) and \((0,1)\): \[\operatorname{Null}(A)=\operatorname{span}\{(-2,1,0,0),\,(-4,0,-1,1)\},\quad \text{nullity}=2.\] (c) Two pivots \(\Rightarrow \operatorname{rank}(A)=2\). (d) \(\operatorname{rank}+\text{nullity}=2+2=4=\) number of columns. ✓
Problem 3  ·  9 points  ·  coordinate map in \(P_2\)

In the space \(P_2\) of polynomials of degree \(\le 2\), a colleague proposes the set \(S=\big(1+x,\;x+x^2,\;1+x^2\big)\) as a "nicer" basis.

  1. Decide whether \(S\) is a basis of \(P_2\). [4 pts]
  2. Find the coordinate vector of \(p(x)=2+4x+4x^2\) relative to \(S\). [3 pts]
  3. Explain in one or two sentences why checking independence of the three coordinate vectors in \(\mathbb R^3\) settles whether \(S\) is a basis of \(P_2\). [2 pts]
Reveal answer
(a) In the standard basis \((1,x,x^2)\) the vectors are \((1,1,0),(0,1,1),(1,0,1)\). Their determinant is \(\det\!\begin{pmatrix}1&0&1\\1&1&0\\0&1&1\end{pmatrix}=2\neq0\), so they are independent; three independent vectors in a 3-dimensional space form a basis. (b) Solve \(a(1+x)+b(x+x^2)+c(1+x^2)=2+4x+4x^2\): \(a+c=2,\;a+b=4,\;b+c=4\Rightarrow a=1,\,b=3,\,c=1\). So \([p]_S=(1,3,1)\). (Check: \(1(1+x)+3(x+x^2)+1(1+x^2)=2+4x+4x^2\).✓) (c) The coordinate map \([\,\cdot\,]_{\text{std}}:P_2\to\mathbb R^3\) is an isomorphism; it sends bases to bases and preserves independence, so \(S\) is a basis of \(P_2\) exactly when its image is a basis of \(\mathbb R^3\).
Problem 4  ·  10 points  ·  subspaces of \(M_{2\times2}\)

An engineer models stress with \(2\times2\) matrices. Let \(W\) be the symmetric matrices and \(U\) the trace-zero matrices, both inside \(M_{2\times2}\).

  1. Show \(W\) is a subspace of \(M_{2\times2}\). [2 pts]
  2. Give a basis and the dimension of \(W\). [3 pts]
  3. Give the dimension of \(U\). [2 pts]
  4. Find \(\dim(W\cap U)\) and then \(\dim(W+U)\) via the dimension formula. What is \(W+U\)? [3 pts]
Reveal answer
(a) The zero matrix is symmetric; if \(A,B\) symmetric and \(c\) a scalar then \((A+cB)^{\mathsf T}=A^{\mathsf T}+cB^{\mathsf T}=A+cB\), so \(W\) is closed under addition and scaling — a subspace. (b) Basis \(\Big\{\begin{psmallmatrix}1&0\\0&0\end{psmallmatrix}, \begin{psmallmatrix}0&0\\0&1\end{psmallmatrix}, \begin{psmallmatrix}0&1\\1&0\end{psmallmatrix}\Big\}\), so \(\dim W=3\). (c) Trace-zero imposes one equation \(a+d=0\) on \(M_{2\times2}\) (\(\dim 4\)), so \(\dim U=3\). (d) \(W\cap U=\) symmetric & trace-zero \(=\begin{psmallmatrix}a&b\\b&-a\end{psmallmatrix}\), \(\dim=2\). Then \(\dim(W+U)=\dim W+\dim U-\dim(W\cap U)=3+3-2=4\), so \(W+U=M_{2\times2}\) (the whole space).
Problem 5  ·  11 points  ·  the fundamental subspaces

A data matrix is \[A=\begin{pmatrix}1&2&1&3\\2&4&3&8\\1&2&2&5\end{pmatrix}.\] Map out its four fundamental subspaces.

  1. Find a basis for the column space \(\operatorname{Col}(A)\) and its dimension. [3 pts]
  2. Find a basis for the row space \(\operatorname{Row}(A)\). [2 pts]
  3. Find a basis for the null space \(\operatorname{Null}(A)\). [3 pts]
  4. Verify rank-nullity, and confirm \(\dim\operatorname{Col}=\dim\operatorname{Row}\). [2 pts]
  5. Find a basis for the left null space \(\{\mathbf y:\mathbf y^{\mathsf T}A=\mathbf 0\}\). [1 pt]
Reveal answer
Row-reduction: \(R_2-2R_1=(0,0,1,2)\), \(R_3-R_1=(0,0,1,2)\); clearing gives RREF \(\begin{pmatrix}1&2&0&1\\0&0&1&2\end{pmatrix}\). Pivots in columns 1 and 3, so \(\operatorname{rank}=2\). (a) Pivot columns of the original \(A\): \(\{(1,2,1),\,(1,3,2)\}\); \(\dim\operatorname{Col}=2\). (b) Nonzero RREF rows: \(\{(1,2,0,1),\,(0,0,1,2)\}\); \(\dim\operatorname{Row}=2\). (c) Free \(x_2,x_4\): \(x_1=-2x_2-x_4,\;x_3=-2x_4\). Basis \(\{(-2,1,0,0),\,(-1,0,-2,1)\}\); nullity \(=2\). (d) \(\operatorname{rank}+\text{nullity}=2+2=4=\#\text{columns}\)✓, and \(\dim\operatorname{Col}=\dim\operatorname{Row}=2\) (row rank = column rank). (e) Rows satisfy \(R_3=R_2-R_1\), i.e. \(R_1-R_2+R_3=0\), so \(\mathbf y=(1,-1,1)\) spans the left null space (dimension \(m-\operatorname{rank}=3-2=1\)).
15:20–16:55 Rank-Nullity Flashcards ~95 MIN · EZ Ch.5.6 · flip & recall

How to use. Read the front, answer out loud or on paper before flipping. Rate each 1–5; anything ≤ 2 goes back into the pile and gets re-tested before you leave the block.

Definition · 01 Define the kernel (null space) of a linear map \(L:V\to W\). tap to flip
Answer \(\operatorname{Ker}(L)=\{v\in V: L(v)=0\}\) — the set of inputs sent to \(0\). It is a subspace of \(V\).
Definition · 02 Define the range (image) of \(L:V\to W\). tap to flip
Answer \(\operatorname{Ran}(L)=\{L(v):v\in V\}\) — all achievable outputs. A subspace of \(W\).
Definition · 03 Define rank and nullity. tap to flip
Answer \(\operatorname{rank}(L)=\dim\operatorname{Ran}(L)\) and \(\text{nullity}(L)=\dim\operatorname{Ker}(L)\).
Theorem · 04 State the Rank-Nullity Theorem. tap to flip
Answer For \(L:V\to W\) with \(\dim V<\infty\): \(\dim V=\operatorname{rank}(L)+\text{nullity}(L)\).
Matrix form · 05 For an \(m\times n\) matrix \(A\), rank + nullity \(=\) ? tap to flip
Answer \(=n\), the number of columns (the dimension of the domain \(\mathbb R^n\)).
Computation · 06 In solving \(A\mathbf x=\mathbf 0\), what does the nullity count? tap to flip
Answer The number of free variables = (columns − pivots) = dimension of the solution space.
Theorem · 07 How do row rank and column rank compare? tap to flip
Answer They are equal — both equal \(\operatorname{rank}(A)\) (= number of pivots).
Theorem · 08 Relate \(\operatorname{rank}(A^{\mathsf T})\) and \(\operatorname{rank}(A)\). tap to flip
Answer \(\operatorname{rank}(A^{\mathsf T})=\operatorname{rank}(A)\) (immediate from row rank = column rank).
Criterion · 09 \(L\) is injective \(\iff\) ? tap to flip
Answer \(\iff \operatorname{Ker}(L)=\{0\}\iff\text{nullity}=0\).
Criterion · 10 \(L:V\to W\) is surjective \(\iff\) ? tap to flip
Answer \(\iff \operatorname{Ran}(L)=W\iff \operatorname{rank}(L)=\dim W\).
Criterion · 11 A square \(n\times n\) matrix \(A\) is invertible \(\iff\) (rank/nullity)? tap to flip
Answer \(\iff \operatorname{rank}(A)=n \iff \text{nullity}(A)=0\) (full rank, trivial kernel).
Inequality · 12 Bound \(\operatorname{rank}(AB)\). tap to flip
Answer \(\operatorname{rank}(AB)\le\min\{\operatorname{rank}(A),\operatorname{rank}(B)\}\).
Subspace algebra · 13 State the dimension formula for subspaces \(U,W\). tap to flip
Answer \(\dim(U+W)=\dim U+\dim W-\dim(U\cap W)\).
Apply · 14 \(A\) is \(5\times3\) with rank 3. Is \(\mathbb R^3\to\mathbb R^5\) injective? surjective? tap to flip
Answer Nullity \(=3-3=0\Rightarrow\) injective. Rank \(3<5\Rightarrow\) not surjective.
Corollary · 15 For \(L:V\to V\) with \(\dim V<\infty\): injective \(\iff\) ? tap to flip
Answer \(\iff\) surjective. By rank-nullity, nullity \(=0 \iff \operatorname{rank}=\dim V \iff\) onto.
Advanced · 16 State Sylvester's rank inequality (\(A\) is \(m\times n\), \(B\) is \(n\times p\)). tap to flip
Answer \(\operatorname{rank}(A)+\operatorname{rank}(B)-n\le\operatorname{rank}(AB)\).
